Sagittarius Shining

Sagittarius A* (Sgr A*) is a supermassive black hole located at the center of the Milky Way galaxy. It is one of the most studied and intriguing astronomical objects, offering insights into the nature of black holes and their role in galactic evolution.

Milkyway

The Milky Way is a vast, barred spiral galaxy that serves as the home of our solar system and countless other stars, planets, and celestial objects. It is one of billions of galaxies in the observable universe. The Milky Way gets its name from the hazy band of light that stretches across the night sky, which is composed of the combined light of countless distant stars.

Solar System

The solar system is a vast and intricate cosmic arrangement consisting of a central star, the Sun, and the celestial bodies that orbit around it. It is a collection of planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and other smaller objects, all bound together by the Sun's gravitational pull. The solar system formed approximately 4.6 billion years ago from a rotating cloud of gas and dust known as the solar nebula. The Sun, a massive ball of hot, glowing gas, forms the heart of the solar system. Its immense gravity not only holds the solar system together but also drives the motion of its planets and other objects.
